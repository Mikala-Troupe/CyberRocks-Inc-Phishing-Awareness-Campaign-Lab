# Lab: CyberRocks Inc. Anti-Phishing Awareness and Training Campaign

This project showcases the design and implementation of a company-wide **Phishing Awareness Campaign** at **CyberRocks Inc.** to reduce phishing incidents, strengthen employee awareness, and improve the organization‚Äôs overall cybersecurity posture.
It follows the **ADDIE instructional design model (Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate)** and demonstrates practical application of **security awareness, behavioral psychology, and incident prevention strategies**.

---

## üß† Objective

The goal of this project was to:

* Reduce phishing click rates among employees
* Increase phishing report submissions
* Improve employee awareness of social-engineering tactics
* Create a sustainable, security-focused culture

The campaign trained employees to identify and report phishing attempts while reinforcing these behaviors through interactive simulations.

## ‚öôÔ∏è Steps Taken

### 1. Analyze

* Reviewed past phishing incidents and employee click data
* Conducted a baseline knowledge survey via HR
* Identified key training gaps and behavioral weaknesses

### 2. Design

* Planned training content using the **ADDIE framework**
* Created a campaign schedule and simulation timeline
* Designed realistic phishing templates for internal testing

### 3. Develop

* Produced short **training videos**, **quizzes**, and **guides**
* Created **posters** and **digital signage** to reinforce awareness
* Built and tested the **‚ÄúReport Phish‚Äù** button for all mail clients

### 4. Implement

* Hosted a kickoff event introducing the new phishing campaign
* Deployed monthly micro-lessons via the LMS
* Conducted controlled **phishing simulations**
* Rolled out the **Report Phish** tool and encouraged usage
* Monitored participation and response metrics

### 5. Evaluate

* Re-surveyed employees and reviewed post-campaign results
* Compared click-rate reduction and reporting frequency
* Collected feedback for future awareness improvements

## üîç Findings

* Interactive training and simulations **significantly reduced** risky clicks.
* The **Report Phish** button increased reporting by 35%.
* Employees demonstrated improved judgment and response to suspicious emails.
* A **blame-free culture** encouraged honest reporting.
* Continuous reinforcement proved more effective than one-time sessions.

---

## üßæ Lessons Learned

* Ongoing, interactive awareness is key for lasting behavior change.
* Regular simulations keep employees alert and confident.
* Collaboration between **IT, HR, and Communications** drives success.
* Visual and practical training is more impactful than long policy emails.
* Each campaign should evolve with new phishing tactics and feedback.
